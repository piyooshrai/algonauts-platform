# Algonaut Revenue Model

## Principle

Students free forever. Companies pay. Advertisers pay.

---

## Revenue Streams

### Stream 1: Company Subscriptions (70% of Revenue)

#### Tier Structure

```
FREE TIER
├── 1 job posting per month
├── View candidate profiles (limited to 10/month)
├── Basic filters only
├── No analytics
└── Purpose: Get them hooked, upgrade later

STARTER - ₹15,000/month ($180/month)
├── 5 job postings per month
├── View unlimited candidate profiles
├── Basic search filters
├── Application tracking
├── Email support
└── Target: Small companies, startups, <50 employees

PROFESSIONAL - ₹35,000/month ($420/month)
├── 20 job postings per month
├── Advanced search filters
├── LayersRank score filtering
├── Candidate comparison tools
├── Priority listing in search
├── Analytics dashboard
├── Dedicated support
└── Target: Mid-size companies, 50-500 employees

ENTERPRISE - ₹75,000+/month ($900+/month)
├── Unlimited job postings
├── API access
├── Bulk hiring tools
├── Custom assessment integration
├── Dedicated account manager
├── Campus partnership management
├── Custom analytics & reporting
├── SLA guarantees
└── Target: Large companies, >500 employees, TCS/Infosys scale
```

#### Pricing Psychology

```
Why these price points:

₹15,000 ($180):
├── Below "needs VP approval" threshold
├── Monthly cost of one bad interview
└── Easy to expense

₹35,000 ($420):
├── Manager approval level
├── Monthly cost of one bad hire's first week
└── Clear ROI story

₹75,000+ ($900+):
├── Procurement process
├── Annual contract (₹9L/year)
├── Compare to: Campus recruitment drive costs ₹5-10L per college
└── We give access to 500+ colleges for same price
```

#### Conversion Funnel

```
Free → Starter: 15% conversion (within 60 days)
Starter → Professional: 25% conversion (within 6 months)
Professional → Enterprise: 10% conversion (within 12 months)

Monthly churn targets:
├── Starter: <8%
├── Professional: <5%
└── Enterprise: <3%
```

### Stream 3: Advertising (10% of Revenue)

#### Ad Placements

```
SKILL GAP ADS
├── Trigger: Student scores low in a skill
├── Placement: "Improve your Python skills"
├── Advertisers: Coursera, Udemy, Scaler, Coding Ninjas
├── Model: CPC ($0.50-2.00) or Revenue share (20%)
└── Example: Score 45 in Python → "Python Mastery Course - 50% off"

CAREER PREP ADS
├── Trigger: Student applying to opportunities
├── Placement: "Ace your interview"
├── Advertisers: Interview prep platforms, resume services
├── Model: CPC ($1-3)
└── Example: Applied to TCS → "TCS Interview Prep Guide"

POST-PLACEMENT ADS
├── Trigger: Student gets placed
├── Placement: Celebration page, follow-up emails
├── Advertisers: Banks (salary accounts), insurance, credit cards
├── Model: CPA ($10-50)
└── Example: Placed at Infosys → "Open your first salary account with ICICI"

SPONSORED LISTINGS
├── Trigger: Browsing opportunities
├── Placement: Featured at top of search results
├── Advertisers: Companies wanting premium visibility
├── Model: CPM ($5-10) or flat fee
└── Example: TCS pays to be first result for "software engineer"
```

#### Advertising Principles

```
RULES:
├── No ads that discourage platform usage
├── No MLM or scam advertisers
├── No predatory lending
├── Student data never sold
├── Ads clearly marked as sponsored
└── Student can opt-out of non-essential ads

TARGETING:
├── Contextual only (based on current action)
├── No behavioral tracking beyond platform
├── No retargeting off-platform
└── Privacy-first approach
```

## Pricing Experiments to Run

```
EXPERIMENT 1: Price Sensitivity
├── Test: ₹12,000 vs ₹15,000 vs ₹18,000 for Starter
├── Metric: Conversion rate, revenue per visitor
└── Hypothesis: ₹15,000 is optimal (below approval threshold)

EXPERIMENT 2: Annual vs Monthly
├── Test: 20% discount for annual vs 10% vs no discount
├── Metric: Annual contract rate, total revenue
└── Hypothesis: 20% discount drives annual without leaving money

EXPERIMENT 3: Free Trial
├── Test: 7-day vs 14-day vs 30-day free trial
├── Metric: Trial-to-paid conversion
└── Hypothesis: 14-day is optimal (enough to see value, urgency to decide)

EXPERIMENT 4: Usage-Based Pricing
├── Test: Per-hire fee ($100 per successful hire)
├── Metric: Revenue, customer acquisition
└── Hypothesis: Per-hire aligns incentives but may limit revenue
```
